Data We Collect

When You Create an Updown Account
In order to use the Updown Apps, you must create an Updown account. When you create an Updown account, we ask for some personal information, including your email address and date of birth. Your email address will be your Updown account ID, and you will use this to log into your account. We do not display your email address to other users. Your birthdate is used to verify that you are at least 13 years of age.

When You Add Information to Your Account
You can customize your Updown experience by adding other types of information to your account, such as entering a workout, adding a photo to your profile, or sending messages to friends. We collect this information as you add it and store it in your Updown account.

When You Visit Our Site
Updown collects industry standard data from everyone who visits our Site. This includes log data that automatically records information about your visit, such as your browser type, operating system, the URL of the page that referred you, the different actions you performed, and the IP address you used to access pages on the Site. We use this type of information to optimize the Site for your benefit and to ensure the Site is working properly.

How We Use Your Data

Updown uses your data to provide you with the best possible experience, to keep you informed about new features, and to improve our Service.
  • Gender and workout data you submit are used by Updown to build personalized workouts that will best meet your needs
  • Contact information (your email address) is used to send you notifications about the latest features so that you can best use Updown for your needs. You may opt out of receiving communication from Updown. Your email address is also used in the case of a forgotten password.
  • Workout data are used in our research to improve the Service
  • De-identified data that does not identify you may be used to inform the fitness and health community about trends, for marketing use, or for sale to interested third parties.

What Data May be Shared with Third Parties

UPDOWN DOES NOT SELL ANY DATA THAT COULD IDENTIFY YOU. We may share de-identified and aggregated data or data that you direct us to share.

Data That Could Identify You
Personal information is data that could identify you such as your name, email address, or other data that could be reasonably traced back to you. We will only share personal information under the following circumstances:
  • When it is necessary to share this information with companies who are contractually obligated to provide us with services such as credit card processing and reward delivery. These companies are required by contract to maintain the security of any personal information they receive from us.
  • If we believe that disclosure of this data is required to comply with the law or when it is in our interest to protect the security of our property or other legal rights or to protect the rights or property of others, or otherwise to help protect the safety or security of our Service.
  • We may disclose or transfer your personal information in connection with the sale, merger, bankruptcy, sale of assets or reorganization of our company. We will notify you if a different company will receive your personal information, and the promises in this Privacy Policy will apply to your data as transferred to the new entity.
De-identified Data
Updown may share or sell aggregated, de-identified data that does not identify you. When we provide this information, we perform appropriate procedures so that the data does not identify you, and we contractually prohibit recipients of the data from re-identifying it back to you.

Data That You Direct Us to Share
We may share data with other parties at your direction. For example, you might authorize us to link your Updown account to your Facebook. Once you direct us to share data with a third party, that data is governed by the third party's privacy policy. You can revoke your consent to share data with a third party at any time in your Updown account settings.

How Long We Save Your Data

We store your personal information for as long as you maintain an Updown account. If you cancel your Updown account, your personal information data will be removed from our server. Updown may continue to use and share your de-identified data after you deactivate your account.

You may edit or remove data that you provide to your Updown account. If you remove data, it will no longer appear to you or others who use the Updown service. Backups of that data will remain associated with your Updown account on our archive servers.

Security of Data

Updown strongly values the security of your data and uses both technical and administrative controls to maintain the security of your data. No data security measures can be guaranteed to be completely effective, and as such we cannot ensure or warrant the security of any information that you provide to us.
